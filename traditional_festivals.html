Traditional Chinese Festivals

Chinese New Year (Spring Festival)

Origin: According to legend, a monster named Nian would attack villagers every Lunar New Year's Eve. To ward it off, villagers used red decorations, loud noises, and fireworks, which remain a tradition today.

Activities: Families gather for reunion dinners, exchange red envelopes, and set off fireworks. Cleaning homes before the new year symbolizes sweeping away bad luck.

Dragon Boat Festival

Story: This festival commemorates Qu Yuan, a patriotic poet who drowned himself to protest corruption. Locals raced boats to save him and threw rice into the river to keep fish away from his body.

Customs: Dragon boat races are held, and people eat zongzi (sticky rice dumplings). Hanging herbs like wormwood wards off evil spirits.

Mid-Autumn Festival

Story: The legend of Chang'e, the moon goddess who ascended to the moon, inspires this festival. People express gratitude for the harvest under the full moon.

Customs: Families gather to eat mooncakes, admire the full moon, and light colorful lanterns, often with riddles written on them.
