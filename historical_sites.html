Explore China's Historical Sites

The Great Wall of China

Introduction: Spanning over 13,000 miles, the Great Wall of China was built to protect against invasions and to unite the country. Construction began during the Qin Dynasty and continued for centuries.

Significance: Symbolizing strength and resilience, the Great Wall is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

The Terracotta Army (兵马俑)

Introduction: Discovered in Xi'an, the Terracotta Army consists of thousands of life-sized clay soldiers, horses, and chariots buried with Emperor Qin Shi Huang to protect him in the afterlife.

Fun Fact: Each soldier has unique facial features, and their arrangement reflects military formations of the time.

The Forbidden City

Introduction: Located in Beijing, the Forbidden City served as the imperial palace for 24 emperors during the Ming and Qing Dynasties. It consists of 980 buildings and is the largest ancient palace complex in the world.

Highlights: The Hall of Supreme Harmony, the emperor's throne, and stunning architectural details like golden roofs and intricate carvings.
