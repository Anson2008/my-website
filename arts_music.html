Arts & Music

Calligraphy (书法)

Calligraphy is one of the highest forms of Chinese art, emphasizing the beauty of written characters. Each stroke reflects the writer's emotions, skill, and rhythm. It's often practiced using a brush and ink on rice paper.

Chinese Painting (国画)

Chinese painting, known as "guohua," uses a brush dipped in black or colored ink. Common subjects include landscapes, flowers, and birds, reflecting harmony between humans and nature.

Peking Opera (京剧)

Peking Opera combines music, vocal performance, mime, dance, and acrobatics. It is a comprehensive art form that originated in the late 18th century and continues to be a symbol of traditional Chinese culture.

Guqin Performance (古琴)

The guqin is a seven-stringed zither that has been played for over 3,000 years. It is considered the most elegant of Chinese instruments and is often associated with scholars and poetry.
